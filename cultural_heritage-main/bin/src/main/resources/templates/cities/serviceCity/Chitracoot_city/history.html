History, Culture, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Chitrakoot, Major Pilgrimage Destination of Uttar Pradesh, India

Ayodhya, the birthplace of Lord Shri Ram and one of the Saptpuri is major pilgrimage center of Uttar Pradesh, India. Ayodhya, also known as Awadh or Awadhpuri or Oudh or Saket, the ancient historical city is headquarter of Ayodhya District and located on bank of holy river Saryu. It hold high religious importance in Hinduism as it is the birth place of Lord Shri Ram, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu and also closely associated with Ramayana the great Indian epic. Thus Ayodhya is famed as one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism. Ayodhya was the capital of the descendants of the Surya dynasty, the legendary Ikshvaku of the ancient Kosala Kingdom and thus also famed as Kaushaldesa.

Ayodhya holds religious importance for Buddhism and Jainism along with Hinduism as it is believed that Lord Gautam Buddha and Lord Mahavir had visited and lived here at Ayodhya. As per the sacred texts of Jainism, Ayodhya is the birthplace of five Tirthankaras namely, Rishabhanatha, Ajitanatha, Abhinandananatha, Sumatinath and Anantnath. Ayodhya is also associate with the legendary king Bharata Chakravarti, the son of Rishabhnatha and after whom the Bharatvarsha named.

Thousands years of history and glory of the region surrounded by Ayodhya has been fallen down in controversy since last five hundred years. It is believed that here in Ayodhya at birth place of Lord Ram, the temple was built which was demolished by the Mughal king Babur and built a mosque there. From that time it was controversy place and later on in December 1992, the mosque was demolition with aim to rebuild a grand temple of Rama at the same site. Recently Supreme Court had ordered to give the land to Hindu Trust to build Ram Mandir. Now the construction of Bhavya Ram Mandir is going on in full fledge and plan to complete by Dec 2023.

There are lots of place of interest here where millions of devotee across the world visits around the year. The main and highly holy place is Shri Ram Janmbhoomi where the grand temple construction is going on. You can also visit famous Ghat locate here on bank of River Saryu, Hanuman Garhi, Nageshwarnath Temple, Treta ka Thakur, Kanak Bhavan etc. You can also plan to visit Chappaiyya which is the birth place of Lord Swaminarayan.

How to Reach Ayodhya, Birth Place of Shree Ram, holiest place of Hinduism, One of Saptpuri, Uttar Pradesh, India

Ayodhya is one of the Seven Sacred site of Hinduism, birth place of Lord Sri Ram and district of state of Uttar Pradesh, India. Thus you can easily reach here via Road, Rail and Air network from across the India. Thousands of devotee and tourist across the world flocked here around the year and once Ram Mandir construction numbers grow to millions every year. Aypdhya is very well connected with all major cities of India via Road, Rail and Air network. The Ram Janmbhoomi is located at just 1 km away from the Ayodhya railway station and you can reach temple from any corner of cities via cab or rickshaw. Ayodhya has major railway junction from where you can get train to all the cities of India like Delhi, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Chennai, Indore, Allahabad, Dehradun etc. at very high frequency. International Airport in Ayodhya has been approved in 2021 so soon you can reach here directly via flight from all major cities of world. Currently you can reach Ayodhya via Lucknow, Prayagraj or Varanasi airport. Also Ayodhya is important district of Uttar Pradesh thus it is well connected by state and national highway. Ayodhya is located at 167 km from Prayagraj, 134 km from Gorakhpur, 135 km from Lucknow, 220 km from Varanasi, 272 km from Chitrakoot.
